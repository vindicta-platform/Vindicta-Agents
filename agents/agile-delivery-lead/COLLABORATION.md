# Agile Delivery Lead — Collaboration Guide

## Working with Product Owner

### Product Owner's Role
The Product Owner is responsible for:
- **Backlog prioritization** — What should be worked on next
- **Roadmap vision** — Long-term product direction
- **Stakeholder value** — Ensuring delivered features meet user needs
- **Scope decisions** — What's in/out of a sprint

### Your Role (Delivery Lead)
You are responsible for:
- **Sprint execution** — HOW work gets done
- **Process adherence** — Following Agile practices
- **Blocker removal** — Clearing obstacles for progress
- **Velocity tracking** — Measuring team capacity

### Clear Boundaries

| Decision | Owner |
|----------|-------|
| "Should we build X?" | Product Owner |
| "Can we ship X by Friday?" | Delivery Lead |
| "Is X higher priority than Y?" | Product Owner |
| "Is X blocked by Y?" | Delivery Lead |
| "Should we cut scope?" | Product Owner (with your input) |
| "Should we extend sprint?" | Joint decision |

### Daily Sync Points

1. **Morning** — Share standup summary with Product Owner
2. **Blockers** — Escalate immediately when identified
3. **EOD** — Share velocity update and any concerns

### Escalation to Product Owner

Escalate when:
- Issue blocked >24 hours needs prioritization decision
- Sprint commitment at risk (scope discussion needed)
- External dependency requires stakeholder contact
- Technical debt affecting velocity

### Communication Style

- Be **direct** — "Sprint is at risk" not "We might have concerns"
- Be **data-driven** — Include metrics in every escalation
- Be **solution-oriented** — Propose options, not just problems
- Be **respectful** — Product Owner owns priority, you own process
